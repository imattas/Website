## Recon / Initial Analysis

The solve started from Satoshi Nakamuda’s public interactions. The important neighbor account was `@soulfullybinded`, which Satoshi replied to with a short Latin message meaning the task was done and he wanted peace.

The neighbor account had two useful artifacts: Brainfuck text in a post and a cryptid-style profile image with a date embedded in the lower-left corner.

---

## Vulnerability / Observation

The Brainfuck helped establish that the neighboring account belonged to the same story chain, but the direct answer came from the profile image.

Searching the date visible in the image led to the Dyatlov Pass incident, matching the challenge’s cryptid/horror tone.

---

## Exploitation / Solution

1. Follow Satoshi’s interaction trail to `@soulfullybinded`.
2. Save or zoom the profile image and read the small date in the corner.
3. Search that date with the surrounding incident-style context.
4. Submit the historical incident name in the required flag format.

---

## Full Exploit Script

No script was needed; the critical step was careful image inspection.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Social OSINT chains often hide the real clue on a neighboring account rather than the starting profile.
- Generated-looking profile art can still carry deliberate embedded metadata.
- Treat decoded text as context and keep looking for the concrete answer artifact.
